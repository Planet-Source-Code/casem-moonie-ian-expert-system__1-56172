GENERAL POLICIES FOR STUDENTS

STANDARD OF CONDUCT



NORMS AND DECORUM

  1. A student of AMA is expected to act in accordance with the accepted norm of behavior in the classroom, laboratories, corridors, canteen and other places inside and within the perimeter of the campus. Courtesy and respect should prevail in dealing and communicating with faculty members, administrative officials, security / maintenance personnel and fellow students. The use of polite language in a vernacular such as “po” or variation of this, cheerful greetings of “Good Morning / Afternoon / Evening are example of politeness, refinement and etiquette.
  2. Student who fail to cooperate with the usual classroom procedures, or those who display acts of disrespect are immediately reported to the Student Affairs’ Office for appropriate disciplinary action.
  3. Silence in passing corridors, ascending the stairs, leaving the class and taking examination should be strictly observed. Stamping of feet, howling, shouting and boisterous laughter should be avoided. 
  4. The black board and chalk and teacher’s tables and chairs are strictly for instructional purposes. Student should refrain from sitting into teachers’s chair / table during vacant hours, before and after class hours. 
  5. Sexual / sensual display of intimacy, public display of affection are strictly prohibited in and within the 500 meter radius of the campus. 
  6. The wearing of earrings is similarly prohibited. Those male students sporting long hair should tie braid or bale their hair to appear neat and groomed.  
  7. An AMACC student is required to maintain the standard of conduct / proper decorum in and outside the College. Any student who does not measure to the expected standard may be requested to withdraw from the College after the disciplinary procedure has been undertaken by the Office of the Student Affairs. 
  8. Cooperation in the upkeep of the buildings and campus is highly anticipated among students. Student should refrain from writing graffiti on walls of classroom / buildings, bulletin board, chairs and other conspicuous places / locations within the campus. Any reported violation should be reported to the Office of the Student Affairs for appropriate disciplinary sanctions. 
  9. The College encourages the use of facilities for purpose it may deem serve, but reserve the right to deny the use of such due to non-compliance to procedures / regulations on the use of facilities. 
  10. Student who want to participate in off-campus undertakings such as contest, conferences and other similar project should initially seek approval of the Dean of Student Affairs through a written communication fostering intention to be involved in such activity.
