POLICY ON SCHOLASTIC DELINQUENCY

RULES GOVERNING SCHOLASTIC DELINQUENCY

 

1. GENERAL PROVISIONS  

1.      The manner of ascertaining academic status shall be based solely on the students’ scholastics performances. Grades in PE and ROTC are excluded.

2.      Passing Mark. A grade of “3” is the minimum passing mark. Grades of “5”, “incomplete “ and “dropped” are to be counted as being below the passing mark.

3.      Maximum Load. Minimum load is to be pegged at fifteen “15” academic units. Student with lower academic load shall be allowed to enroll on case to case basis as approved by the Dean.

4.      The 18 Unit Rule. Any student who failed to pass a minimum of eighteen (18) academic unit in one school year apply to students who have been allowed to enroll with lower academic loads than aforementioned prescribed minimum.

5.      The Maximum Residency Rule. Any students who fail to complete any  academic requirement in his / her course within a period of actual residence equivalent to 1 ½ times the normal length prescribed for the course shall not be allowed to register further in the College. A four year courses a maximum residency of 14 trimester while a five year course has a minimum residence of 16 trimesters. This rule does not apply if the student is on approval leave of absence.

2. STUDENT ON NOTICE (N)

1.      Any student who obtains a final grades below three (3) in more than 25% and less than 50% of the total numbers of     academic units for which he is register will receive a notice from the Dean to improve his work  

2.      Said student shall be considered as having improve his/her work after passing more than 50% of total number of academic units in the next trimester.

3. PROBATION (P)

1.         Any student obtains final grades below 3 in more than 50% and less than 75% of the total of academic units in which he has final grades shall be placed on probation for the succeeding trimester and his load shall be limited to the extent as to be determined by the Dean.

2.         Any student who shall receive a notice from the Dean to improve his/her work two (2) successive trimester shall also be placed on probation for the succeeding trimester.

3.         If the student has a total number of more than 12 academic units in the previous trimester in which he / she to be placed on probation shall be lessened by one subject.

4.         If the student has total number of academic unit which is equivalent to or less than 15 units, the total number of  academic units to be taken for the trimester in which he/she  is to be placed on probation shall be lessened by one subject.

5.         A student is to be released from probationary status upon passing more than 75% of the total number of the academic units.

4.) dismissal (ds)

1.         Any student, who at the end of the trimester, obtains final grade below three and more than 75% but less than 100% of the total number of academic units in which he receives final grades shall be dropped from the rolls of the College.

2.         Any student, on probation who fails 75% or more than the total number of academic units in which he receives final grades shall be dropped from the rolls of the College.

3.         A student dropped from the rolls of College may be readmitted with the approval of the Vice Chancellor within three (3) trimester with the condition that he/she must pass all the academic load.

4.         To determine the total number of academic units to be taken by the student previously dismissed and readmitted, the rules of academic rules on academic load of a student on probation status shall apply.

 5.) DISQUALIFICATION (DQ)

  1. Any student who, at the end of the trimester obtained final grades below three (3) in the 100% of the academic units in which he is given final grades shall permanently barred from readmission to the College.

  2. A student previously dismissed but readmitted and fails to pass more than 75% of the academic load shall be permanently  barred from readmission to the College.

     Permanent disqualification does not apply to cases were in recommendation of   the instructors concerned, the faculty certify that the grade of 5 is due to the students unauthorized dropping of the subjects and not to poor scholarship. However, if the unauthorized withdrawal takes  place after the mid trimester and the student’s standing is poor, his grades of 5 shall be counted against him for the purpose of this scholarship.
