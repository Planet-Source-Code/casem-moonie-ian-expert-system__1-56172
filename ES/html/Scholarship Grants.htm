SCHOLARSHIPS, HONORS AND GRADUATION AWARDS

SCHOLARSHIP GRANTS

 

AMACC FOUNDATION SCHOLARSHIP FOR ACADEMIC EXELLENCE

The program is open to both incoming freshmen and continuing students with at least one (1) year of residency. It covers full waiver in the tuition and other fees. One grantee is selected for every 250 students. Incoming freshmen student qualifies by passing the competitve examinations. Continuing students must have at least a cumulative GWA of 1.75. The numberof awardees is largely based on the student enrollment population for a given term. Preference is given to those with family income below P60, 000.00

The AMACC Scholarship Program is being implemented to comply with the requirement of providing scholarship grants for one student per 500 students (1:500) as set forth by Presidential Decree No. 451.

QUALIFICATION AND REQUIREMENTS

For Incoming Freshmen Students:

The parents or guardian/s of the applicant must have an annual gross income not exceeding P 60,000.00. Annual gross income includes the income of both parents or of all the guardians. Guardianship should be manifested through an affidavit. All other qualifications regarding this matter should also be manifested through an affidavit.

The applicant must submit a Certificate of Good Moral Character.

The applicant must not be presently enjoying any form of financial assistance r scholarship grant.

The applicant must not be related to any of the officers or faculty members within the fourth civil degree by consanguinity.

The applicant should have taken and passed the Competitive Scholarship Examination with a minimum over-all percentile rating of 40%.

The applicant should have finished secondary education at the top ten percent of the graduating class with a compositionof not less than 200 members.

For Continuing Students:

The parents or guardian/s of the applicant must have an annual gross income not exceeding P 60,000.00. Annual gross income includes the income of both parents or of all the guardians. Guardianship should be manifested through an affidavit.

The applicant must submit a Certificate of Good Moral Character.

The applicant must not be presently enjoying any form of financial assistance r scholarship grant.

The applicant should have obtained a cumulative general weighted average of 1.75 or better, must be up-to-date with all the academic and non-academic requirements; and must have no grade lower than 2.5 in any of his/her academic subjects and 3.00 in any of his/her non-academic subjects.

The applicant must have been enrolled in the college for at least three consecutive trimester with a minimum load of 15 units each trimester.

The applicant must not be related to any of the officers or faculty members within the fourth civil degree by consanguinity.

HIGH SCHOOL VALEDICTORIANS AND SALUTATORIANS

This scholarship grant shall cover waiver in tuition fees. Valedictorian shall be given 100% while Salutatorians shall be given 50% waiver in tuition fees. Grantee shall be a student who has successfully completed secondary education in any DECS recognized private or public secondary school with a graduating class of at least 200 members.

REQUIREMENTS

Accomplished application form;

Certification from the High School Principal that the applicant is;

2.1 a valedictorian or salutatorian of a HS graduating class

2.2 of good moral standing

Passed the AMACC Entrance Examination;

Enrolling within the first year of the academic program which he/she has selected.

 

DURATION AND RENEWAL

The grant shal be for one (1) trimester and subject to renewal each succeeding term upon the fulfillment of the following requirements:

The grantee must have enrolled all required academic and non-academic subjects during the previous and subsequent terms;

A cumulative generl wieghted average (CGWA) of 1.75 or better to avail of 100% waiver in tuition fees;

No grade lower than 2.5 in any academic or non-academic subject;

Good moral standing during grant;

Submission of certificate of grades from College’s Registration to the Officer of the Den of Student Affairs;

Accomplished Renewal of Scholarship Form.

STUDENT LEADER SCHOLARSHIP

The student Council President, the Editor-Chief-Chief of DATALINE, and Corps Commander shall enjoy tuition fe waiver for one school year upon fulfillment of all of the following requirements:

GWA of at least 2.5 during the previous term with no failing grade in any subject;

Compliance with the rules on minimum load of 15 units.

Good moral stading and have not been found guilty of any offense by the Office of the Student Affairs.

SPECIAL SCHOLARSHIP GRANTS

Philippine veterans who are iligible for inancial aid under the Republic Act No. 65 are eligible for admission upon presentation of their eligibility certificate issued by the Philippine Veterans Board subject to compliance with the admission requirements.

US Veterans who are entitiled to educational benefits under the US.

Servicemen’s Readjustment Act are eligible for admission upon presentation of their eligibility certificate from the local US Veterans Administrative Board provided they must meet the requirements for admission.

Presidential decree No. 577 provides that dependents of military Personel who die or become incapacitated in the line of duty are to be awarded fre tuition and matricuation fees upon submission of the necessary proof and ducoments from the Pension and Maturity Administration Office of the Armed Forces of the Philippines.

EXTERNAL SCHOLARSHIP GRANT

The College honors grants directly subsidized either by the government or by a private entity. A grantee should immediately present to the Office of Student Affairs all documents for the early processing of his scholarship papers.
