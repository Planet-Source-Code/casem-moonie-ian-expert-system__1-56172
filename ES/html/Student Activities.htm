GENERAL POLICIES FOR STUDENTS

STUDENT ACTIVITIES

 

Organizer of Student activities must secure a permit from the Dean of Student Affairs at least seven (7) days before the scheduled activity. The Dean may regulate as so time, place and manner such activities in order to ensure that normal academic function shall not be disputed.

SOURCE VERIFICATION OF POSTERS / TEASERS / LEAFLETS

            The College recognizes the student’s freedom of expression. However, to ensure the responsible ventilation of views, opinions and the like, students are required to have the source of posters, teasers and similar items verified at the OSA.

GUIDELINES ON STUDENT ACTIVITIES

            The College firmly believes in developing the full potential of each individual, nurturing his leadership capability and fostering the spirit of camaraderie and unity among fellow forms groups and aggradations to be able to actively participate and subsequently enjoy their temporary stay in the Institutions. The following are the implementing guidelines:

1.      Proposed student activity should be coursed through the Office of the Student Affairs a week before the actual event. If the activity is academic, it requires for prior approval from the College Dean concerned. The proposal is in the form of written communication furnishing the OSA a file for filing reference purposes.

2.      Any monetary/pecuniary collection that the organization undertakes requires the approval/supervision of the Office of the Student Affairs. If the organization is engaged in ticket selling, it is required the ticket should be properly stamped at the Office of the Student Affairs taking into account the number as reflected in the proposal and the expenses incurred plus the intended amount for a particular project to be implemented.

3.      Students engage in such activities requiring the use of facilities shall coordinate with the Director for facilities and security services in writing informing and seeking his approval a week prior to the intended event.

4.      Outside activities require the submission of waiver to be duly signed by parents or guardians and consequently forwarded to the Office of the Student Affairs a week before the activity. A formal letter addressed to the Office of the Student Affairs must contain the following: itinerary of activity to include the names of student who wish to join, purpose and financial requirement.

5.      A one (1) week moratorium on all co-curricular and extra-curricular activities prior to the calendared dates of the Mid Term and Final Exams shall be strictly observed to allow student enough time to prepare and review for the exam at hand.

SELLING OF TICKETS/SOLICITATIONS

     Any group which intends to sell tickets or solicit contribution as part of a fund raising activity must initially secure a written approval from the Dean of Student Affairs. In the same vein, solicitation of any kind by faculty members from the students is strictly prohibited.
