GUIDELINES ON DISCIPLINARY ACTIONS

SANCTIONS

 

The Manual of Regulation of Bureau of Private Schools, 1992, 8th edition, Annotated, Article 14, section 77 describes the four (4) categories of disciplinary administrative sanction which maybe imposed on erring students commensurate with the nature and gravity of the violation of school rules and regulations as follows:

1.      DROPPING. A school may drop from the role during the school year or term a student who is considered undesirable. The student who is dropped should immediately be issued his transfer credentials.

2.      SUSPENSION. A school may suspend an erring student during the school year or term for maximum period not exceeding 20% of the prescribed schooldays. Suspension which will involve the loss of the entire year or term shall not be effective unless approved by the Director or Private School.

3.      EXPULSION. The penalty of an expulsion is an extreme form of administrative sanctions which debars the student from all Public and Private schools. To be valid and effective, the penalty for expulsion requires the approval of Commission on Higher Education (CHED). Expulsion is usually considered proper punishment for gross misconduct or dishonesty and/or such offenses as hazing, carrying deadly weapons, immorality, drunkenness, vandalism, hooliganism, assaulting a teacher or any school authority or his agent or student, instigating, leading to stoppage of classes, preventing or threatening the students or faculty members or school authorities from discharging their duties, or from attending classes or entering school premises, forger ring or tampering school records or transfer forms, or securing or using such forged transfer credentials.

4.      EXCLUSION. This is a penalty in which the schools is allowed to exclude or drop the name of the student from school enrollment list for being undesirable, the school may release transfer credentials.
