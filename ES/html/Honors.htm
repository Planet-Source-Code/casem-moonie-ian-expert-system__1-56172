SCHOLARSHIPS, HONORS AND GRADUATION AWARDS

HONORS
 
1. TRIMESTRAL HONORS

Superior scholastic achievement is recognized through the publication of the President’s and Dean’s Lists at the end of each trimester. To qualify, a student must have:

  1. Carried a minimum load of 15 units for the term;
  2. A GWA of 1.45 or better to qualify for President’s list; a GWA of 1.46 to 1.75 to qualify ofr Dean’s list, as certified by the Office of the Registrar;
  3. No grade lower than 2.5 in any subject;
  4. No been found quilty of any offense by the Student Disciplinary Tribunal (SDT) or the Offfice of the Student Affairs.

Students who qualify for Trimestral Honors shall be made available at the Registrar’s Office. 

President’s and Dean’s Lister  are automatically members of the Honor’s Society (an elite organization of academically excellent   students(. They may also qualify in the AMACC Foundation Scholarship Program for Academic Excellence as continuing scholar.

     The Honors and Awards Committee headed by the Registrar shall call for the nominations to the graduation awards by sending a notice to the academic community. The Registrar will convene a committee for the deliberation, selection and recommendations of the awardees.

     Students who are candidates for honors must have completed at least seventy-five (75%) of the total number of academic units or hours of AMACC. Full time students who are candidates for honors should have taken not less than fifteen (15) units. The rule shall not apply during the last trimester of their enrollment. Working students who are candidates for honors must have taken not less than nine (9) units of credits in academic subjects each trimester. The candidates must possess good moral character and must not have been subjected to disciplinary action shall be is disqualified to receive academic honors.
