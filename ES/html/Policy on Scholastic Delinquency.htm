Note:  NO “NFE” GRADE POLICY

 

            Student who failed to take the final examination shall be given a grade of NFE in a period of seven (7) working days to take a special examination. C. GRADE COMPLAINTS

           

            Complaints on grade obtained shall be entertained only within a period of one week after the posted schedule for grade distribution. Grade complaints will not be entertained after said period.

 

 

D. EXAMINATIONS GUIDELINES

           

            The following testing rules / procedures should be observed during examination:

  1. Wear ID properly.
  2. Bring your examination permit and clearly write the subject and section at the back of this document. A student shall not be allowed to take the examination without the permit.
  3. Be sure that the proctor signs the permit during the examination period.
  4. Place all the things except examination documents in front of the room.
  5. Come on time. Latecomers may be allowed to take the examination provided they will not extend the examination period.
  6. Honesty is always the best policy. Cheating shall be punished with a grace of 0 percentage in the examination.
  7. Leaving the room during examination shall not be allowed. Attend to your personal needs before the examination starts.
  8. Ensure that you sign the attendance sheet (2 copies) for each subject taken.

 

 

All the conflicts in examination schedule should be settled within the examination week. Schedule of the examination for subjects with conflicts will be prepared and announced by the area Coordinator concerned. Students must notify their respective teachers of said conflict/s one week before the scheduled exam. No exam shall be administered to students who failed to notify their teachers about the conflict in schedule.

 

Midterm exam in the laboratory is scheduled a week after the midterm examination week and the final exam is scheduled a week before the finals.

 

E. APPLICATIN FOR ID CARD, ID VALIDATION

 

            All the students must secure identification cards from the Registrar’s Office. Student must present Identification Cards when entering the school premises properly worn at all times. The student must always be the basis for identifying a students when he transacts business with the College.

 

            In case of loss of the ID card, a student should execute an affidavit of loss and submit same to the Registrar’s Office. Student pays an amount of P50.00 to the Accounting Office and present the receipt to the Registrar’s Office with his 1” x 1” picture (red background).

 

F. GRADUATION

           

            The institution confers degrees and titles only to a student who has satisfactorily completed all the academic requirements in the curriculum based from the student’s record in the office of the Registrar.

 

I. EVALUATION

           

            Student files application for evaluation of academic credit prior to the last term of his course. The evaluator checks, verifies, evaluates all the record of the student. Results of the evaluation are posted in the bulletin board before the last enrolment of the student.

 

 

2. DELIBERATION

 

A preliminary deliberation shall be conducted when the midterm grades are available and the final deliberation after the submission of final grades for students. List of candidates indicating deficiencies shall be posted in the bulletin board after the deliberation.

 

 

G. STANDARD OF CONDUCT

 

NORMS AND DECORUM

 

  1. A student of AMA is expected to act in accordance with the accepted norm of behavior in the classroom, laboratories, corridors, canteen and other places inside and within the perimeter of the campus. Courtesy and respect should prevail in dealing and communicating with faculty members, administrative officials, security / maintenance personnel and fellow students. The use of polite language in a vernacular such as “po” or variation of this, cheerful greetings of “Good Morning / Afternoon / Evening are example of politeness, refinement and etiquette.

 

  1. Student who fail to cooperate with the usual classroom procedures, or those who display acts of disrespect are immediately reported to the Student Affairs’ Office for appropriate disciplinary action.

 

  1. Silence in passing corridors, ascending the stairs, leaving the class and taking examination should be strictly observed. Stamping of feet, howling, shouting and boisterous laughter should be avoided.

 

  1. The black board and chalk and teacher’s tables and chairs are strictly for instructional purposes. Student should refrain from sitting into teachers’s chair / table during vacant hours, before and after class hours.

 

  1. Sexual / sensual display of intimacy, public display of affection are strictly prohibited in and within the 500 meter radius of the campus.

 

  1. The wearing of earrings is similarly prohibited. Those male students sporting long hair should tie braid or bale their hair to appear neat and groomed.

 

  1. An AMACC student is required to maintain the standard of conduct / proper decorum in and outside the College. Any student who does not measure to the expected standard may be requested to withdraw from the College after the disciplinary procedure has been undertaken by the Office of the Student Affairs.

 

  1. Cooperation in the upkeep of the buildings and campus is highly anticipated among students. Student should refrain from writing graffiti on walls of classroom / buildings, bulletin board, chairs and other conspicuous places / locations within the campus. Any reported violation should be reported to the Office of the Student Affairs for appropriate disciplinary sanctions.

 

  1. The College encourages the use of facilities for purpose it may deem serve, but reserve the right to deny the use of such due to non-compliance to procedures / regulations on the use of facilities.

 

  1. Student who want to participate in off-campus undertakings such as contest, conferences and other similar project should initially seek approval of the Dean of Student Affairs through a written communication fostering intention to be involved in such activity.
